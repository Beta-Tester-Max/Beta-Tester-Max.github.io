PRIVACY POLICY

2025

MSWDO ("we," "us," or "our") respects your right to privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us through the Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situations (AICS) website .

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you access and use our website.

Information We Collect
When you visit or use the AICS website, we may collect personal information such as your full name, date of birth, address, contact details like your phone number and email address, government-issued identification numbers, family and household details, employment and income information, and supporting documents related to medical, death, or emergency situations. We may also automatically collect technical information such as your IP address, browser type, device information, date and time of visit, and the pages you accessed.

How We Use Your Information
The information you provide is used to assess and process your application for assistance, to communicate with you regarding your application, to generate reports required by government agencies, to improve the security and functionality of our website, and to comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

How We Share Your Information
We may share your information with authorized government agencies and partner institutions strictly for verification and service delivery purposes. We may also share your information with service providers who perform tasks on our behalf under confidentiality obligations. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. Disclosure of information may also occur when required by law, subpoena, or other legal process.

Data Retention
Your personal data will be retained only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy or as required by applicable law. Afterward, your information will be securely deleted or anonymized.

Data Protection and Security
We employ appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. However, while we strive to protect your information, no method of internet transmission or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your Rights Under the Data Privacy Act
You have the right to access the personal information we hold about you, to request corrections to any inaccurate or incomplete data, to withdraw your consent to the processing of your data, to object to certain types of data processing, to request deletion or blocking of your personal data, to request the portability of your data, and to file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission if you believe your rights have been violated. If you wish to exercise any of these rights, you may contact us at MSWD Alaminos FB or MSWD email
