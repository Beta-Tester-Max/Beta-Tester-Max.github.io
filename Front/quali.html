Transportation Assistance Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

a. Must be a resident in the municipality

b. Must demonstrate an urgent need for transportation due to a crisis situation (medical condition, rescue of abused, court hearing, death, and critical situations of family members)

c. Must belong to a low-income or financially distressed household

Specific Crisis Situations Covered

Applicants may qualify for transportation assistance if they fall under any of the following categories:

a. Medical-Related Emergency

  • Individuals requiring specialized medical treatment in another location due to the unavailability of necessary healthcare services in their current area
  • Patients needing referral or transfer to a specialized medical facility for further treatment
  • Individuals needing medical evacuation (e.g., air or sea transport) for life-threatening conditions
  • Persons requiring emergency transportation to a medical facility

b. Humanitarian Situations

  • Individuals or families displaced due to natural disasters, armed conflict, or other emergencies and needing transportation back to their home province or safe location
  • Survivors of human trafficking or other forms of exploitation who need transportation for rescue, reintegration, or legal proceedings
  • Individuals traveling to attend court hearings related to legal matters such as child custody, domestic abuse, or violation of protection orders for their dependents

c. Family and Personal Emergencies

  • Individuals needing to travel due to the death of an immediate family member (e.g., spouse, parent, child, or sibling)
  • Individuals who must urgently attend to a critical health situation of a close family member in another location
  • Individuals needing to assist in the rescue or recovery of a missing, kidnapped, or trafficked relative

Food Assistance Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • Must demonstrate an urgent need for food assistance due to financial hardship, disaster, or crisis
  • Households with unemployed or underemployed breadwinners
  • Individuals or families with no regular source of income
  • Displacement or armed conflict requiring immediate food support
  • Senior citizens, persons with disabilities, or other vulnerable groups

Qualifying Crisis Situations

Applicants may be eligible for food assistance if they fall under any of the following categories:

a. Economic Hardship

  • Individuals or families who are financially incapable of providing food for themselves
  • Households with unemployed or underemployed breadwinners
  • Families experiencing adverse economic difficulties due to loss, illness, or sudden financial crisis

b. Disaster or Emergency Situations

  • Victims of natural disasters such as typhoons, earthquakes, floods, or landslides
  • Victims of fire incidents, displacement, or armed conflict requiring immediate food support
  • Evacuees or individuals in temporary shelters due to calamities

c. Health and Nutrition

  • Malnourished children and individuals suffering from severe undernourishment
  • Pregnant or lactating mothers in nutritional distress
  • Patients requiring special nutrition or food assistance due to financial incapacity

d. Social Welfare and Protection Cases

  • Homeless individuals and street dwellers in need of nourishment
  • Victims of domestic abuse, human trafficking, or other forms of exploitation under the care of social welfare
  • Orphans, abandoned children, or elderly persons under temporary protective custody

Medical Assistance Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • Must demonstrate an urgent medical need requiring financial assistance
  • Patients unable to afford essential medical expenses, medications, or medical procedures due to financial hardship
  • Patients with no health insurance or insufficient coverage for the required medical expenses

Qualifying Medical Situations

Applicants may be eligible for medical assistance if they are experiencing any of the following:

a. Emergency Medical Conditions

  • Life-threatening conditions requiring immediate hospitalization (e.g., heart attack, stroke, severe infections, organ failure)
  • Accident-related injuries requiring emergency medical intervention (e.g., fractures, burns, head trauma)
  • Surgical procedures that require immediate urgency to prevent deterioration of health

b. Critical Illness and Chronic Disease Support

  • Individuals diagnosed with critical or life-threatening illnesses (e.g., cancer, kidney failure, chronic heart disease, diabetes complications)
  • Patients requiring long-term medication or lifesaving implants (e.g., pacemakers, prosthetics)
  • Individuals in need of costly laboratory tests, imaging procedures, or specialized treatments for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes

c. Financially Distressed Patients

  • Patients unable to afford hospitalization expenses, medicines, or medical procedures due to financial hardship
  • Individuals from low-income families who require medical assistance to continue essential treatment
  • Patients with no health insurance or insufficient coverage for the required medical expenses
❤️

Cash Relief Assistance Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • Must demonstrate a clear need for financial assistance due to a fire incident, calamity, or other emergency situations
  • Families who have no permanent homes, sources of income, or essential belongings
  • The family belongs to a low-income household
  • Survivors of domestic violence, abuse, or human trafficking needing financial relief

Qualifying Crisis Situations

Applicants may be eligible for cash relief assistance if they are affected by any of the following:

a. Natural and Man-made Disasters

  • Fire incidents resulting in the loss of home, livelihood, or essential belongings
  • Natural disasters such as typhoons, earthquakes, floods, landslides, or volcanic eruptions
  • Man-made disasters such as armed conflict, terrorist attacks, or structural collapse leading to displacement
  • Evacuees or individuals in temporary shelters due to calamities

b. Sudden Economic Hardship Due to an Emergency

  • Individuals whose sole source of income was lost due to a disaster (e.g., farmers affected by drought, workers displaced by factory closures)
  • Families whose primary breadwinner has died or suffered severe injury or disability due to an emergency
  • Households experiencing sudden financial hardship due to medical emergencies, funerals, or other unforeseen crises

c. Vulnerable and At-Risk Individuals

  • Families with young children, pregnant or lactating mothers, elderly members, or persons with disabilities
  • Single-parent households or families headed by minors
  • Survivors of domestic violence, abuse, or human trafficking needing financial relief
  • Individuals in protective custody, shelters, or temporary housing due to a crisis situation

Burial Assistance Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

  • The applicant must be a legal spouse, child, parent, or legal guardian of the deceased
  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • The family must demonstrate financial incapacity to cover funeral expenses
  • The family belongs to a low-income household

Qualifying Crisis Situations

Applicants may be eligible for burial assistance if the death occurred under any of the following circumstances:

a. Financial Hardship

  • The family belongs to a low-income household and cannot afford burial expenses
  • The family has no access to insurance, pension, or other financial aid for funeral costs
  • Deceased individuals who were dependent on the municipality for basic needs

b. Death Due to a Disaster or Calamity

  • Death caused by a natural disaster (e.g., typhoon, earthquake, flood, landslide, fire)
  • Death caused by a man-made disaster (e.g., armed conflict, vehicular accident, structural collapse)
  • Multiple casualties resulting from a single disaster

c. Death Due to a Critical Event or Emergency

  • Death caused by accidents, sudden medical emergencies, or violence (e.g., homicide, fire, drowning, electrocution)
  • Cases where the body needs repatriation from another city, province, or country

Educational Assistance Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • The student must be enrolled in a recognized educational institution (elementary, high school, senior high school, or university)
  • The student must be a student-in-crisis facing financial hardship that hinders their ability to continue schooling
  • Must not be a recipient of municipal scholarship program or other similar government-funded educational assistance programs
  • A maximum of one (1) student per family is eligible for this assistance
  • The student must belong to a low-income or financially distressed household

Qualifying Crisis Situations

Applicants may be eligible for educational assistance if their family is facing significant financial hardship due to any of the following:

a. Economic Hardship

  • Students belonging to a family without financial support
  • Children of parents with disabilities who are unable to effort school expenses
  • Children of unemployed parents with no stable source of income
  • Children in foster care or under protective custody

b. Health-related Crises

  • Students who have Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) or are living with parents with HIV
  • Students who need medical attention or hospitalization that disrupts their education

c. Victims of Abuse or Displacement

  • Students who are victims of domestic violence, neglect, or abuse
  • Children displaced due to disasters, armed conflict, or eviction

Psychosocial Support Qualifications

Criteria of Eligibility:

  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • Must be experiencing emotional, psychological, or social distress due to a crisis situation
  • Must be willing to undergo psychosocial interventions, including counseling, therapy, or support programs

Qualifying Crisis Situations

Applicants may be eligible for psychosocial support if they are affected by any of the following:

a. Trauma and Emotional Distress

  • Individuals experiencing grief, loss, or emotional breakdown due to the death of a loved one, separation, or abandonment
  • Survivors of violent crimes, accidents, or sudden traumatic incidents
  • Individuals suffering from extreme anxiety, depression, or post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) following a crisis

b. Disaster and Emergency Situations

  • Victims of natural disasters (e.g., typhoons, earthquakes, fires, floods) leading to psychological distress
  • Survivors of man-made disasters (e.g., armed conflict, forced eviction, community violence)
  • People who have been displaced or relocated due to calamities, conflicts, or demolition

c. Abuse and Violence-Related Cases

  • Victims of physical, emotional, or sexual abuse, domestic violence, or neglect
  • Individuals suffering from bullying or discrimination, including those from marginalized groups
  • Victims of human trafficking, forced labor, or exploitation

d. Mental Health Concerns

  • Individuals suffering from severe stress, anxiety, depression, or other mental health concerns affecting their daily life
  • Persons who engage in self-harm, substance abuse, or suicidal ideation due to crisis situations
  • Families with children, elderly members, or persons with disabilities (PWDs) experiencing emotional or social distress
